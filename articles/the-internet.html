The Internet

Ooftypop

Published on: February 7, 2019

The internet is the technical infrastructure which makes the web possible. Namely, it is the collection of hardware and protocols used to facilitate the communications, and transportation of data, between connected devices.

The “highways” that traffic these communications and data are constructed of cable, fibre, radio waves, and other, creative, methods to signal information, even including the use of producing frequency patterns with visible light.

Beyond the web applications and email that you’re already familiar with, the internet is being used increasingly more to bring what were once boring objects to life, in the form of lights that can change color based on the health of your investments, or sprinkler systems that can learn when the best time is to water the crops, or watches that can alert your doctor when one of your vitals is irregular.

The internet has even begun to expand beyond Earth, with satellites sending and receiving the web’s data, making up a portion of the technical infrastructure, and with the astronauts on the ISS emailing their spouses, and live-streaming video to the world.

The creation of the internet has had far, and deep, reaching effects on societies, effects which have permeated nearly every aspect of the modern world, bringing about a new age of humanity--the age of information.

Some people think of the internet as the nervous system of humanity, the convergence of all individuals’ thoughts and dreams and behaviors, and the beginning to something even bigger.
